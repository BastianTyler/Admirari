Introduction to Linear Algebra

Linear algebra is the branch of mathematics concerning linear equations, linear functions, and their representations through matrices and vector spaces. Here's an example:

Matrix Multiplication

To multiply two matrices, we perform the following operation:

$$ \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = \left[ \begin{matrix} a_{11} & a_{12} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} \end{matrix} \right] \cdot \left[ \begin{matrix} b_{11} & b_{12} \\ b_{21} & b_{22} \end{matrix} \right] $$

Where \( \mathbf{A} \) and \( \mathbf{B} \) are matrices, and the result of multiplication is another matrix.

Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues

The eigenvalue equation is given by:

$$ \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{v} = \lambda \cdot \mathbf{v} $$
