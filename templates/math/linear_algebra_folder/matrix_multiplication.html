Matrix Multiplication

This page covers the basics of matric multiplication

Scalar Multiplication

Multiplication by a scalar is easy, just distribute it in.

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 8\\ 3 & 4 & 9\\ 5 & 6 & 7\\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$ $$ 5A = \begin{bmatrix} 1*5 & 2*5 & 8*5\\ 3*5 & 4*5 & 9*5\\ 5*5 & 6*5 & 7*5\\ \end{bmatrix} \quad = \quad \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 10 & 40\\ 15 & 20 & 45\\ 25 & 30 & 35\\ \end{bmatrix} \quad

Matrix x Matrix Multiplication

This gets a bit more tricky.

The rule for when we can multiply is when the the leading matrix's column number matches the following matrix's row number.

The result will be a matrix that is the same dimentions as the leading matrix's row number and the following matrix's column number.

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 8\\ 3 & 4 & 9\\ 5 & 6 & 7\\ \end{bmatrix} \quad B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 3\\ 2 & -1\\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$

Notice matrix A is 3 rows x 3 columns and matrix B is 3 rows x 2 columns. A's column number and B's row number match, thus we can multiply them!

What do you expect the resulting matrix to be? Well 3 x 2 of course. As corresponding to A's column number and B's row number

But How Do We Multiply?

The algorithm is as follows:

Starting with the first row of A, take each element and multiply it by the corresponding element of the first column of B

Then sum each of those products together to get the first entry in the new AB matrix.

Repeat with the same row of A for every column of B

Once finished with each column of B for that row, go the next row in A an redo the process

Once all of A's rows have been processed you are done

$$ AB = \begin{bmatrix} ( 1*1 + 2*0 + 8*2 ) & ( 1*0 + 2*3 + 8*-1 )\\ ( 3*1 + 4*0 + 9*2 ) & ( 3*0 + 4*3 + 9*-1 )\\ ( 5*1 + 6*0 + 7*2 ) & ( 5*0 + 6*3 + 7*-1 )\\ \end{bmatrix}\quad $$ $$ AB = \begin{bmatrix} 17 & -2\\ 21 & 3\\ 19 & 11\\ \end{bmatrix}\quad $$

Some things to remember

  1. BA != AB. Matrix Multipication is not communitive
  2. Look at the dimensions of the matrix side by side to easily tell if they can be multiplied.
