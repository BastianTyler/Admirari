Intro to the Inverse

This page will describe the basic properties of Inverse matricies and how to calculate them using the identity matrix

What is the Inverse?

The inverse is simply what brings a matrix to the identity matrix.

Not every matrix has an inverse. If it does, then we say that the matrix is invertable.

Think if this as how we can essentially divide out a matrix

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -3 & 3 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & 3 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & -1 \\ \end{bmatrix} $$

Matrix B is the inverse of matrix A. To check lets multiply them!

$$ AB = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -3 & 3 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & 3 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & -1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$ $$ = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} $$

Wow! We got the identiy. We should also check BA to be sure, but I leave that up to you... good luck!

Finding the inverse with the identity matrix

So how do we find the inverse? Well, if there is one, we can make an augmented matrix of the matrix we want the inverse of and of the identity.

We start with matrix A

Our goal is to transform Matrix A into the identity matrix. Each row operation we perform on A should occur on the identiy. We augment A to make this obvious.

And the resulting matrix should be A inverse! We can check this by multiplying the original A by the inverse of A.
