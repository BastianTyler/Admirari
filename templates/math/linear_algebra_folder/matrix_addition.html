Matrix Addition

Matrix addition is the operation of adding two matrices by adding the corresponding elements together. The two matrices must have the same dimensions to be added together.

Example of Matrix Addition

Consider the following two matrices:

$$ A = \left[ \begin{matrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{matrix} \right], \quad B = \left[ \begin{matrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{matrix} \right] $$

To add them, simply add the corresponding elements:

$$ A + B = \left[ \begin{matrix} 1+5 & 2+6 \\ 3+7 & 4+8 \end{matrix} \right] = \left[ \begin{matrix} 6 & 8 \\ 10 & 12 \end{matrix} \right] $$

The result of the addition is:

$$ C = \left[ \begin{matrix} 6 & 8 \\ 10 & 12 \end{matrix} \right] $$

Steps for Matrix Addition
  1. Ensure that both matrices are the same size (same number of rows and columns).
  2. Add the corresponding elements from both matrices.
  3. Write the resulting matrix by combining the summed elements.
