Matrix Addition

This page has some basic explanations and examples for matrix addition

When can we add?

We can add when two matricies have the same dimensions.

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 3 \\ -4 & 9 \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$

Here A and B are the same m x n dimentions, that is they are both 3 x 2 in size. So we can add them!

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad C = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 8\\ 3 & 4 & 9\\ 5 & 6 & 7\\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$

Here A and C are not the same m x n dimentions. A is 3 x 2 in size and C is 3 x 3. So we can't add them.

Example of adding A + B:

Given: $$ A + B = D $$ $$ D = \begin{bmatrix} 1+1 & 2+0 \\ 3-2 & 4+3 \\ 5-4 & 6+9 \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$

The result is:

$$ D = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 2 \\ 1 & 7 \\ 1 & 15 \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad $$
