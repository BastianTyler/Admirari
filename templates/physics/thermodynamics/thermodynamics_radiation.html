Radiation Intro

Radiation is essentially the rate at which any object will naturally emit energy. Like conduction, we discus radiation in terms of Power, \(mathcal{P}\). As such we can again relate the power output to the equation: $$\mathcal{P} = \frac{dQ}{dt} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta T}$$ as discussed in Conduction.

Some things we should note before going on:

  • Temperature for radiation should best be discussed in units of Kelvin.
  • What surface do we care about? The 3d area between objects: that is the surface area of the object.
Formulas For Radiation

There are three primary formlas to care about (but they are hardly different)

First: $$\mathcal{P}_{radiated} = \sigma A_{surface-area}\epsilon T_{object-radiating}^4$$

Second: $$\mathcal{P}_{absorbed-from-enviorment} = \sigma A_{surface-area}\epsilon T_{enviorment}^4$$

Third: $$\mathcal{P}_{net} = \sigma A_{surface-area}\epsilon (T_{enviorment}^4-T_{object-radiating}^4)$$

Notice, the only thing changing is that final T value. All we are saying is that the surface area of some 3D object is touched by the surface area of the enviorment it is in; therefore there is some sort of exchange going on. We descibe each, the object radiating power in question and the power the object feels thanks to the enviorment it is in in the first two formulas. The third finds the net power, of say a person in an elevator, when taking the ambient power from the enviorment into account.

Cool, so what are all these symbols?

  • \(\sigma\)
  • \(A_{surface-area}\)
  • \(\epsilon \)
