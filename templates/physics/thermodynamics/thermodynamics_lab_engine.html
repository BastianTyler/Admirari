Mass Lifter Heat Engine

Analysis and algebraic solutions for the experiment.

Process Identification

A → B: Isothermal compression (cold gas, adding mass)

B → C: Isobaric expansion (hot gas, mass present)

C → D: Isothermal expansion (hot gas, removing mass)

D → A: Isobaric compression (cold gas, mass removed)

Pressure Inside the Piston

Using Newton’s Second Law:

$$ P_{\text{inside}} A = mg + P_0 A $$

Solving for \( P_{\text{inside}} \):

$$ P_{\text{inside}} = P_0 + \frac{mg}{A} $$

With cross-sectional area \( A \):

$$ A = \frac{\pi D^2}{4} $$ $$ P_{\text{inside}} = P_0 + \frac{4mg}{\pi D^2} $$
Total Volume of Air

Total volume includes the can and piston air:

$$ V_{\text{total}} = V_{\text{can}} + V_{\text{piston}} $$

Where:

$$ V_{\text{can}} = \frac{\pi D_1^2}{4} h_1 $$ $$ V_{\text{piston}} = \frac{\pi D_2^2}{4} h_2 $$

Final expression:

$$ V_{\text{total}} = \frac{\pi}{4} \left( D_1^2 h_1 + D_2^2 h_2 \right) $$
Work to Lift 150g

Work to lift mass:

$$ W = mgh $$

Where \( m = 0.150 \) kg and \( g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2 \).

PV Diagram Work

Approximated as a parallelogram:

$$ W_{\text{gas}} = \text{width} \times \text{height} $$

Convert volume to L and pressure to kPa: \( 1 \) kPa·L \( = 1 \) J.

Percent Difference
$$ \% \text{Difference} = \frac{|W_{\text{gas}} - W_{\text{lift}}|}{W_{\text{gas}}} \times 100 $$
Adiabatic Process Check
$$ P_1 V_1^\gamma = P_2 V_2^\gamma $$

Solving for \( V_2 \):

$$ V_2 = V_1 \left( \frac{P_1}{P_2} \right)^{\frac{1}{\gamma}} $$
Isothermal Process Check
$$ \frac{P_A V_A}{P_B V_B} = \frac{n_A R T_A}{n_B R T_B} $$

Since the process is isothermal, \( T_A = T_B \) and \( R \) cancels out:

$$ \frac{P_A V_A}{P_B V_B} = \frac{n_A}{n_B} $$

Solving for \( V_B \):

$$ V_B = V_A \left( \frac{P_A}{P_B} \right) \left( \frac{n_A}{n_B} \right) $$
Efficiency of the Heat Engine
$$ \eta = \frac{|W_{\text{net}}|}{|Q_{\text{hot}}|} $$
Power Output
$$ P = \frac{W_{\text{net}}}{t} $$

where \( t = 30 \) s.
