Thermodynamics: Conduction

Heat flow with objects in contact

Conduction Intro

Conduction is the heat flow of two solid objects in contact. A steak and a hot pan, your feet and the cold floor, a can of chili warming up in a pot of boiling water; you get the idea.

Things to be aware about

Conduction cares about the flow of heat through some object. As such it cares about the cross sectional area of an object. Towards the end of this page there is a list of some common cross sectional areas you should know.

Heat flows from HOT to COLD it does not flow from COLD to HOT.

What is thermal equallibrium? It is when heats balance, so that is when heats sum to 0.

What does it mean to be in steady state? It implies that for the system in question the rate of heat production has settled to a constant value. This will be the case in many problems.

Formula for power over time

Remember this: we can always relate power to change in heat over change in time!

Formula:

$$\mathcal{P} = \frac{dQ}{dt} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta T}$$ Where:

  • \(\mathcal{P}\) is power
  • \(\text{dQ} = \Delta Q\) is change in heat
  • \(\text{dt} = \Delta t\) is change in time. Take note that this is NOT temperature

Note on \(\Delta T\): Remeber that this is \(t_f - t_i\). Later we will see looking at change in temperature like this helps find the junction of two bodies.

General Formula For Conduction

When can we apply this? The bodies must be solid and in physical contact.

Formula:

$$\mathcal{P} = \frac{k_bA_{cross-sectional-area}\Delta T}{L} $$ Where:

  • \(\mathcal{P}\) is power
  • \(k\) is the thermal conductivity of the material. This changes for each material.
  • \(A_{cross-sectional-area}\) is, risking being overly on the nose, the cross sectional area of the object. We will discuss this in detail later on
  • \(\Delta T\) is change in temperature. Note we can use Kelvin or Celsius
  • \(L\) is the length of the material in question
R-Value

The R value is the thermal resistance of a object. The formula for R is: $$R = \frac{L}{k}$$

Basic Problem Solving Strategy

  1. Write down values and their associated quantities.
  2. Bring them into correct units.
  3. Identify target value
  4. Use power and heat formulas to get target values.
