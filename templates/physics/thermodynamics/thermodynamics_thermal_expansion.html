Thermodynamics: Thermal Expansion

General idea of thermo

How does the dimensions of an object change under a change in temperature?

The expansion of an object is classified into several catagories.

  • Linear Expansion
  • Area Expansion
  • Volumetric Expansion

Note that an object will expand in all dimensions at the same rate when undergoing thermal expansion

Linear Expansion

Linear expansion occurs in respect to single dimensions of an object, but occurs equally in all directions. This could be the linear length of a rod, or it's height, or even be a spheres diameter!

The formula for the change in length due to linear expansion, \(\Delta L\), is estimated by: $$\Delta L = L_o\alpha\Delta T$$ where

  • \(\alpha\) Coeffiecient of Linear Expansion
  • \(L_o\) Initial Length, this could be diameter.
  • \(\Delta T\) Change in temperature

After expansion we should expect: $$L_f = L_o + L_o\alpha\Delta T$$ $$L_f = L_o(1 + \alpha\Delta T)$$

Volumetric Expansion

Volumetric, or volume expansion, behaves similarly to linear expansion. The formula modeling it is $$\Delta V = V_0\beta\Delta T$$ where

  • \(\beta\) Coeffiecient of Volume Expansion
  • \(V_o\) Initial Volume
  • \(\Delta T\) Change in temperature

For Solids we will use: $$\beta = 3\alpha$$

Area Expansion

Area expansion is given by the formula $$\Delta A = A_02\alpha\Delta T$$ where

  • \(\alpha\) Coeffiecient of Linear Expansion
  • \(A_o\) Initial Area
  • \(\Delta T\) Change in temperature

Worked example: The Metal Sphere and Ring
