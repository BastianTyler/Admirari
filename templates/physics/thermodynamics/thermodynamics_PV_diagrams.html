Thermodynamics

General idea of thermo

Pv Diagram Intro

A PV diagram for a gass allows us to calculate the work of that gas as some state variables change. Supposing we defiend two points A & B to correspon to the state of the gas at two points in time we could calculate the area under A to B to get the work by the gas. This calculation is depending on the type of Process the gas is undergoing. Note the graph does have direction as indicated by arrows.

$$W_{\text{by gas}} = \int_{i}^{f}Pdv = area under the curve$$

Isochoric

A straight vertical line. The area under any such line is 0, so the work must be 0. Also reffered to as isovolumetric.

Process: V is constant

Condition

$$W_{\text{by gas}} = \int_{i}^{f}Pdv = 0 $$

First Law

$$\Delta E_{\text{int}} = Q_{\text{in}}$$

Isobaric

A horizonal line. Imagine the area as a box. Then the work is simply the width x height, or for a PV diagram, Pressure x Change in Volume.

Note the direction of volume change. If the volume shrinks, work by gas is negative. If it expands, work by gas is positive.

Process: P is constant

Condition

$$W_{\text{by gas}} = \int_{i}^{f}Pdv = P\Delta V $$

First Law

$$\Delta E_{\text{int}} = Q_{\text{in}} - W_{\text{by gas}}$$

Isothermal

Not straight, and occurs when there is constant temperature. This can happen because heat is being exhanged with its surroundings equally. The change in internal energy is 0 as T itself is a measure of internal energy. No change in T no change in \(\Delta E_{\text{int}}\)

Process: T is constant

Condition

$$W_{\text{by gas}} = \int_{i}^{f}Pdv = PVln\frac{V_f}{V_i} = nRTln\frac{V_f}{V_i} $$

First Law

$$\Delta E_{\text{int}} = 0 $$

Adiabatic

Similar to isothermal processes, but because there is no heat exhange between the system and the surroundings we say Entropy, S, is constant and that the process is Adiabatic. Because Q is missing, the change in internal energy is just opposite of the work done by the gas.

Process: S is constant

Condition

$$W_{\text{by gas}} = \frac{1}{1-\gamma}(P_fV_f - P_iV_i) $$ $$Q_{\text{in}} = 0 $$

First Law

$$\Delta E_{\text{int}} = - W_{\text{by}}$$
