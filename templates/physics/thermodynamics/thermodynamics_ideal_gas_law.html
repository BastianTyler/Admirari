Intro to the Ideal Gas Law

General idea of thermo

The Ideal Gas Law

Formula: $$ PV = nRT \text{ or } PV = Nk_bT $$ where:

  • P- Absolute Pressure in units \(Pa\) = \(\frac{N}{m^2}\)
  • V- in units \(m^3\)
  • T- Temperature in units \(K\), dont't use \(^\circ C\)
  • n- Number of moles. No units.
  • N- Number of molecules. Often a very large number. No units.
  • R- in units \(\frac{J}{molK}\)
  • \(k_b\)- Boltzmann Constant = \(\frac{R}{N_A}\) = 1.381 x 10^-23 \(\frac{J}{K}\)

Key Assumptions

  1. Huge number of molecules.
  2. Molecules move in random directions with random speeds.
  3. Molecules spend most of that time far apart from one another.
  4. Colisions are elastic, thus energy is conserved.
  5. Intermolecular forces are negligible except durring collisions.
  6. Molecules use up negligible volume of container
Moles and Molar Mass

A mole is matches the atomic weight of an atom. 1 mole = 1 mol = \(N_a\) elementary units

There are two formulas too worry about: $$ n = \frac{N}{N_A} \text{ or } n = \frac{m}{\mathcal{M}} $$ where \(m\) mass of material is and \(\mathcal{M}\) stands for molar mass.

Molar Specific Heat

Affected by conditions, so specific types of molar heat are specified for each problem with subscripts

Constant Volume Process $$Q = nC_v\Delta T$$ where:

  • Q is the heat required to rais the temperature of 1 mole of material by 1 K = 1^\circ C and assumes gas does not change volume while heated
  • \(C_v\) is the molar specific heat at constant volume in units of \(\frac{J}{molK}\)

Constant Pressure Process $$Q = nC_p\Delta T$$ where:

  • Q is the heat required to rais the temperature of 1 mole of material by 1 K = 1^\circ C and assumes gas does not change pressure while heated
  • \(C_p\) is the molar specific heat at constant pressure in units of \(\frac{J}{molK}\)
